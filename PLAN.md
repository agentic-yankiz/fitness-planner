# 🏋️ Shaked's Strength Plan — One-Arm Pull-Up · Planche · Lean

A **4-week wave**: each week gets harder, **Week 4 is the peak**, then a **deload**
week, then you start the cycle again with slightly better numbers.

> Goals: one-arm pull-up, planche, lose fat — **strength-first, no running.**
> Read this page to train. Deeper "why" + every video is in [`knowledge/`](knowledge/).

---

| Day | Focus | What |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | 🧗 **Boulder** | Limit bouldering (hard, long rests) + light shoulder warm-up |
| **Tue** | 💪 **Strength A — Pull** | One-arm pull-up work + **14 mm repeaters** + planche skill + shoulder prehab |

**Every session starts with the 8-min warm-up** (wrists + shoulders) — see bottom of page.

## How each week gets harder

You keep the **same exercises** for all 4 weeks. You only change **how hard** the
main lifts are. Effort is on the **RPE** scale (RPE 8 = 2 reps left in the tank).

| | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 (peak) | Week 5 (deload) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Effort** | RPE 7 | RPE 8 | RPE 8–9 | RPE 9–10 | RPE 5–6 |
| **Weighted pull-up load** | base | +2.5 kg | +5 kg | +7.5 kg | base −20% |
| **Max hang load** (14 mm, Fri) | base | +2.5 kg | +5 kg | +7.5 kg | base −20% |
| **Repeaters** (14 mm BW, Tue) | base sets | +1 set | +1 rep / set | +1 rep / set | half the sets |
| **Skill holds** (planche / lock-off) | base time | +2 s | +4 s or harder lean | +6 s or next level | half the sets |
| **Assisted OAP** | most help | less help | less help | least help / negatives | most help |
| **Sets on main lifts** | as written | +0 | +1 set | +1 set | −half |

After Week 5 deload → restart at Week 1 with **new, higher base numbers** (the loads
and times you hit in Week 3–4 become your new baseline).

## 🧗 Boulder days (Mon & Thu)

Keep climbing — it's training. Just give the two days different jobs.

| Day | Job | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | **Power / limit** | After warm-up: hard problems at/near your max (V5–V7). Long rests (2–4 min). 60–90 min. Quality over quantity. |
| **Thu** | **Volume / technique** | Moderate problems (V3–V5), focus on movement, footwork, flow. More climbing, shorter rests. 60–90 min. |

- **Light shoulder warm-up before every session** (it's part of the 8-min warm-up).
- **No hangboard on climbing days.** Dedicated finger work (max hang, repeaters) now lives on the two strength days — see Tue & Fri. This keeps max finger work off the limit-boulder days so fingers and elbow tendons aren't loaded hard back-to-back.

## 🤏 Finger strength — hangboard (two days/week, at home)

Long-term goal: **a one-hand hang on a 20 mm edge, both sides.** That's a big finger-strength
jump from where you are (two-hand +20 kg on 14 mm), so it's a slow, patient build that
feeds straight into the one-arm pull-up.

| Day | Session | What | Why here |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fri** (Strength B) | **Weighted max hang** — 14 mm | 5 × 7–10 s near-max, 2–3 min rest | Heaviest finger work, done **fresh** on a push day — clear of the Tue one-arm day and Mon limit boulder |
| **Tue** (Strength A) | **Repeaters** — 14 mm, bodyweight | 3 × (7 s on / 3 s off) × 6, 2–3 min rest | Submaximal capacity work; a finisher that won't steal the one-arm pulling |

- **One edge, two jobs:** max hangs build raw strength; repeaters build tissue capacity.
- **Progress max hangs by load** (the wave adds ~2.5 kg/week), repeaters by adding a rep or set.
- **Path to the 20 mm one-hander:** two-hand max hang → assisted one-hand (feet / pulley) → one-hand. Don't chase the one-arm hang until two-hand max hangs feel easy.
- **Elbow rule:** medial-elbow or finger-tendon ache → cut load, never push through. Same tissue the one-arm work hammers.

Full protocol, progressions, and the one-hand pathway: [`knowledge/finger-strength.md`](knowledge/finger-strength.md).

## 🔥 Fat loss — no cardio needed

You lose fat in the **kitchen**, keep muscle in the **gym**. No running required.

- **Eat in a small deficit:** ~300–500 kcal/day under maintenance. Slow and steady.
- **Protein:** **150–185 g/day** (1.8–2.2 g/kg). Hit this first, every day.
- **Walk:** aim ~8–10k steps/day. That's your "cardio" — it isn't really cardio.
- **Optional Saturday density finisher** (strength-style, not running): 10–15 min of
  kettlebell swings / loaded carries / sled — see [`knowledge/fat-loss-nutrition.md`](knowledge/fat-loss-nutrition.md).

### Rules of thumb
- **Pain-first rule:** front-shoulder pain above ~3/10 → stop that exercise, do the prehab, see the physio notes.
- **Skill before grind:** do planche/one-arm skill work *fresh*, early in the session.
- **Don't stack fingers:** max hangs live on Friday, repeaters stay submaximal on Tuesday, and a hard limit-boulder Monday means **lighten or skip** Tuesday's repeaters — never run max finger + max one-arm pulling on back-to-back days.
- **Log every session** in [`tracking/`](tracking/) so next cycle starts from real numbers.
- Two heavy days + two climbing days is plenty — **more is not better** when the goal is one-arm pull-up and planche.
