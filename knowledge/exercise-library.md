## 1 · Fingers / Hangboard

### Wrist rotation
*Spreadsheet: "Wrist rotation"*

**Goal:** Warm-up wrists and forearms before any finger work.
**Cues:** Full range; slow and controlled; light dowel or bare hands; both directions.
**Progress:** Add a light dumbbell. **Regress:** Passive wrist circles only.
**Video:** [Dowel Wrist Rotation — Forearm strengthening exercise for stronger wrists](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/h6l0KQfZbKs)

---

### Weighted max hang 14 mm
*Spreadsheet: "Max hang 14 mm (weighted)"*

**Goal:** Raw finger strength → foundation for one-arm hang and OAP.
**Cues:** Half-crimp grip; dead hang; scapulae packed down; weight on dip belt; no swinging; precise hold time (7–10 s).
**Progress:** Add ~2.5 kg per week in the wave; progress path → 20 mm edge → assisted one-hand hang.
**Regress:** Remove weight; move to 20 mm edge.
**Videos:**
- [Hangboard Edge Hang — Half Crimp](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gY6AwopLSJc)
- [Hangboard Training for Max Finger Strength](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFMGbHIewTs) — Chris Hailey
- Full protocol: [`knowledge/finger-strength.md`](finger-strength.md)

---

### Hangboard repeaters 14 mm (bodyweight)
*Spreadsheet: "Bodyweight repeaters (14 mm)"*

**Goal:** Finger endurance and tissue capacity; submaximal volume on top of strength hangs.
**Cues:** 7 s on / 3 s off × 6 reps; half-crimp; bodyweight only; leave 2–3 reps in reserve; scapulae set.
**Progress:** Add a rep per set, then add a set. **Regress:** Fewer on-cycles; larger edge.
**Video:** [Bodyweight Repeaters | Intermediate Hangboard Session | Lattice Training Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P6F-vEE0D9w) — Lattice Training
**Reference:** [Hangboarding routine (Hooper's Beta)](https://www.hoopersbeta.com/library/hangboarding-routine-training-for-climbing)

---

### Isometric hang (weighted)
*Spreadsheet: "Isometric hang (weighted)"*

**Goal:** Hanging pulling-chain strength (not just fingers); direct OAP support.
**Cues:** Two-hand dead hang; weight via dip belt; precise hold time; shoulders packed; no body movement.
**Progress:** Increase weight or extend hold time. **Regress:** Reduce weight; bodyweight.
**Video:** [ISO 90 Hang](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KOnBLaYuji8)

---

### Pinch block hold
*Spreadsheet: "Pinch block hold"*

**Goal:** Pinch grip strength for climbing; complements crimp-dominant hangboard work.
**Cues:** Squeeze all fingers around the block; neutral wrist; don't torque the implement; hold steady.
**Progress:** Add weight; increase hold time. **Regress:** Lighter or narrower block.
**Video:** [Gods Of Grip — Top Tips For Improving Your Pinch Lifting](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eJ7fLOvBNm8)

---

### Wrist curls
*Spreadsheet: "Wrist curles"*

**Goal:** Wrist flexor and forearm strength; elbow armour for heavy pulling.
**Cues:** Forearm supported on bench; full ROM curl and extend; slow eccentric; light load.
**Progress:** Increase load; add reps. **Regress:** Lighter dumbbell; reduce ROM.
**Video:** [Personal Fitness Tips: How to Build Big Wrists by Using Dumbbells](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u61QWKYgbxI) *(generic demo; not a preferred channel)*

---

### Wrist extension
*Spreadsheet: "Wrist extension"*

**Goal:** Wrist extensor strength; flexor/extensor balance to protect medial elbow.
**Cues:** Forearm supported; use light weight (4 kg); controlled lowering phase; full ROM.
**Progress:** Increase load. **Regress:** Bodyweight; passive stretch.
**Video:** [How To Do Wrist Extensions](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UcwgCTkVvIQ)

## 2 · Pull

### One-arm pull-up assisted ("OAP")
*Spreadsheet: "One-arm pull-up assisted" / "Oap"*

**Goal:** Primary one-arm pull-up skill work; training the specific movement pattern.
**Cues:** Ring or band assist; keep elbow tracking back (not flaring); full ROM; don't rotate torso; controlled lowering.
**Progress:** Reduce assistance weight; progress through the 16-level ladder. **Regress:** More assistance; two-hand pull-up.
**Videos:**
- [Assisted one hand ring pull up](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/-tU_6jcFBPQ) *(spreadsheet reference)*
- [How to One-Arm Pullup (in 16 Levels of Difficulty)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b5gp3QB3rMU) — Hooper's Beta *(full progression map)*

---

### Archer pull-up
*Spreadsheet: PLAN.md Day A*

**Goal:** Unilateral pulling strength; direct OAP progression.
**Cues:** One arm pulls (working arm), other arm extends straight (assisting); hips stay square; no torso rotation; full ROM on working side.
**Progress:** Reduce help from the extended arm; eventually true one-arm. **Regress:** Band on straight arm; lower overall load.
**Video:** [How-To: Archer Pull-Up [5 Progressions Tutorial]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_LGLKUiQH5k)

---

### One-arm negatives
*Spreadsheet: PLAN.md Day A (included in 16-level OAP video)*

**Goal:** Eccentric overload for OAP; trains the hardest portion with controlled lowering.
**Cues:** Jump or use two hands to reach top position; one hand on bar; lower 5–8 s; elbow stays in; fully extend at bottom.
**Progress:** Slow the descent; reduce assistance getting to the top. **Regress:** Band assist for lowering; 3-s descent.
**Video:** [How to One-Arm Pullup (in 16 Levels of Difficulty)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b5gp3QB3rMU) — Hooper's Beta

---

### One-arm lock-off holds
*Spreadsheet: PLAN.md Day A (included in 16-level OAP video)*

**Goal:** Isometric OAP strength at top and 90° angle; strengthens the hardest portions of the pull.
**Cues:** Hold at chin-above-bar (top) or 90° elbow bend; shoulder blade down and back; don't shrug; breathe.
**Progress:** Increase hold time; add small weight. **Regress:** Band assist; shorter hold.
**Video:** [How to One-Arm Pullup (in 16 Levels of Difficulty)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b5gp3QB3rMU) — Hooper's Beta

---

### Lock-off 120°
*Spreadsheet: "Lock-off 120°"*

**Goal:** OAP-specific isometric at 120° arm angle — often the weakest position.
**Cues:** Arm at 120° from full extension; shoulder blade depressed; chest up; hold tight.
**Progress:** Increase duration; then move target angle toward 90°. **Regress:** Band assist; shorter hold.
**Video:** [120 degree lock off](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/2D_Xb_ucH_o)

---

### Lock-off (bodyweight)
*Spreadsheet: "Lock-off (bodyweight)"*

**Goal:** General OAP isometric strength (top or mid-range).
**Cues:** Hold at top of pull-up or 90° bend; scapulae depressed; no shrug; breathe.
**Progress:** Add small weight; longer hold. **Regress:** Band; shorten hold.
**Video:** [Lock off](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/nl_fszFMVbM)

### Chin-up iso (weighted)
*Spreadsheet: "Isometric chin-up hold" / "Chin-up iso (weighted)"*

**Goal:** Heavy isometric pulling strength to complement finger iso hangs; drives OAP.
**Cues:** Chin at or above bar; weight via dip belt; hold position tight; scapulae packed.
**Progress:** Increase weight; increase hold time. **Regress:** Bodyweight; reduce hold time.
**Video:** [Isometric Chin-up/Pull-up Hold](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/WuFtS0xNwmI)

### Chest chin-up (weighted)
*Spreadsheet: "Chest chin-up (weighted)"*

**Goal:** Main pulling compound for OAP; full-ROM supinated-grip pull-up.
**Cues:** Supinated (underhand) grip; pull sternum to bar; elbows track backward; depress scapulae on the way up; full lockout at bottom.
**Progress:** Add weight per wave; transition toward archer and OAP. **Regress:** Remove weight; use band.
**Video:** [Proper Chin-Up Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mRy9m2Q9_1I)

### Chest pull-up (weighted)
*Spreadsheet: "Chest pull-up (weighted)"*

**Goal:** Full-ROM pronated pull-up to chest; trains the hardest final inches of the rep.
**Cues:** Pronated (overhand) grip; pull until chest approaches bar; retract scapulae at top; control the lowering.
**Progress:** Add weight; move to OAP work. **Regress:** Band; reduce load; reduce ROM.
**Video:** [❌ Beginner Pull Up Mistake!](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/IZMKe61144w) *(demonstrates that stopping short of the chest is the error — teaches full-ROM by negative example; valid cue reference)*

### Single-arm row
*Spreadsheet: "Single-arm row"*

**Goal:** Horizontal pulling strength; scapular retraction; elbow health.
**Cues:** Chest to pad or anchor point; row with elbow tracking close to body; squeeze scapula at top; neutral spine throughout.
**Progress:** Increase weight; add pause at top; archer row. **Regress:** Reduce weight; shorten ROM.
**Video:** [Single Arm Ring Rows](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w4Jhh82Ilos)

### Inverted weighted rows (rings)

**Goal:** Horizontal pulling with elevated difficulty; full scapular retraction under load.
**Cues:** Body straight; pull chest to rings; shoulders packed throughout; pause at top; weight via vest or plates.
**Progress:** Increase weight; steeper body angle. **Regress:** Less weight; flatter body angle.

### One-arm scapular pulls
*PLAN.md Day B (Hörst reference)*

**Goal:** Scapular depression under one-arm load; foundational OAP movement.
**Cues:** One hand on bar; pull shoulder blade down without bending elbow; hold 1–2 s at bottom; don't shrug.
**Progress:** Add pause; progress to partial one-arm pull. **Regress:** Two-arm scapular pulls.
**Video:** [The Best Climbing Exercise You're Not Doing!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6P9r0UAdwXM) — Hörst / TrainingForClimbing

### Eccentric straddle front lever
*Spreadsheet: "Eccentric straddle front lever"*

**Goal:** Front lever and lat strength; transitions into the resistance-band straddle front lever.
**Cues:** Start from inverted hang; slowly lower to horizontal (3–5 s); straddle legs (wider = easier); keep body rigid; lats engaged throughout.
**Progress:** Slow the descent further; narrow legs; add a hold at horizontal. **Regress:** Advanced tuck position; shorter range of motion.
**Video:** [Eccentric Straddle Front Lever](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fnLJ24Eg6I)

---

### Resistance band straddle front lever / Straddle front lever
*Spreadsheet: "Resistance band straddle front lever" / "Straddle front lever (no band)"*

**Goal:** Front lever isometric hold; direct lat and core strength for climbing and OAP.
**Cues:** Hips at shoulder height; body parallel to floor; lats locked; band assists hips; don't pike; hold position for prescribed time.
**Progress:** Remove band; narrow leg straddle; increase hold time. **Regress:** Thicker band; wider straddle; use tuck position.
**Video:** [FRONT LEVER TUTORIAL | How to Master the Front Lever](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e_YwP0O34zM) *(straddle progression at 4:34)*

## 3 · Push / Planche

### Planche lean
*Spreadsheet + PLAN.md*

**Goal:** Primary planche skill; builds protraction strength and anterior deltoid load tolerance.
**Cues:** Wrists below hips; lean forward until shoulders are in front of hands; scapulae protract (push floor away); body stays straight; don't pike.
**Progress:** Lean further forward; increase hold time; progress to tuck planche. **Regress:** Reduce lean angle.
**Video:** [How To Planche Lean Correctly | Calisthenics Beginner Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wKV5zVJTYBo)

---

### Tuck planche hold
*Spreadsheet + PLAN.md*

**Goal:** First full planche position; all body weight supported on straight arms.
**Cues:** Hips directly above wrists; tuck knees tight to chest; protract maximally; arms perfectly straight; don't pike.
**Progress:** Raise hips; extend tuck; move toward advanced tuck → straddle. **Regress:** Planche lean; reduce tuck height.
**Video:** [HOW TO TUCK PLANCHE](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M5il8Jpltt8)
**Straddle path:** [From TUCK to STRADDLE PLANCHE in 30 days](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IP-Vs4jfSyc)

---

### Pseudo planche push-ups
*Spreadsheet + PLAN.md: "Pseudo / planche push-ups"*

**Goal:** Planche strength with dynamic movement; builds protraction endurance under load.
**Cues:** Push-up position with hands behind hips; lean forward into planche position; lower chest; maintain forward lean throughout the rep.
**Progress:** More forward lean; eventually true planche push-ups. **Regress:** Less lean; standard push-up position.
**Video:** [How To Planche Push-Up (BEST PROGRESSIONS)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TZ63httkob4) — FitnessFAQs

---

### Scapular protraction push-ups
*Spreadsheet + PLAN.md: "Serratus / scap push-ups", "Serratus wall slides"*

**Goal:** Serratus anterior and scapular protraction strength; critical for planche; also protects the front shoulder.
**Cues:** At top of push-up, push floor away and round upper back; hold 1–2 s at top; control lowering; don't let shoulder blades pinch together.
**Progress:** Ring protraction push-ups; add weight vest; increase reps. **Regress:** Wall push-up level; bodyweight only.
**Video:** [Master the Planche: The Best Guide to Correcting Scapula Protraction](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1uQxbgwA7-0)

---

### L-sit to tuck planche
*Spreadsheet: "L-sit to tuck planche"*

**Goal:** Dynamic planche skill; bridges compression strength (L-sit) and horizontal hold (tuck planche).
**Cues:** Start in L-sit; shift weight forward; protract scapulae as hips rise into tuck; hold each position 3 s; control transition.
**Progress:** Increase hold time at each position; faster transitions; add reps. **Regress:** Practice each position separately before combining.
**Video:** [L sit to PLANCHE tuck](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/VGMQb0fyaHA)

---

### Advanced tuck raises
*Spreadsheet: "Advanced tuck raises" (also appears as "Advanced Tuck Front lever raises")*

**Goal:** Core compression and front lever pulling strength; prerequisite for tuck lever holds.
**Cues:** From dead hang; compress knees tight to chest; raise hips toward parallel; control the lowering; don't swing.
**Progress:** Get more parallel on raise; hold at top; transition to tuck front lever. **Regress:** Basic knee raises; bent-leg raises.
**Video:** [Advanced Tuck Front lever raises](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/GQsm9V5L5LA)

### Rings push-up
*Spreadsheet: "Rings push-up"*

**Goal:** Chest and tricep pressing with ring instability; stabiliser recruitment for planche.
**Cues:** Keep rings turned out at bottom; turn in at top lockout; stay tight throughout; elbows track back.
**Progress:** Feet elevated; add weight; decline angle. **Regress:** Rings at higher (more vertical) position; reduce ROM.
**Video:** [Best Push-ups for CHEST GAINS?!](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/kBuvYdndV_k) *(demonstrates ring push-up variation; title is generic)*

### Elevated pike push-up
*Spreadsheet: "Elevated pike push-up"*

**Goal:** Overhead pressing bodyweight; handstand push-up progression.
**Cues:** Feet elevated; hips high; elbows track back; lower nose between hands; perpendicular upper arm at bottom.

### Dips (weighted)
*Spreadsheet: "Dips (weighted)"*

**Goal:** Horizontal pressing compound; triceps and anterior deltoid; supports planche.

## 4 · Hinge / Squat

### Barbell box back squat
*Spreadsheet: "BB box back squat"*

**Goal:** Lower-body strength; hip strength that transfers to climbing power and posterior chain.
**Cues:** Sit back onto box; knees track over toes; maintain neutral spine; box stops at parallel — no lower; come to full stop before returning.
**Progress:** Add weight; eventually remove box. **Regress:** Goblet squat; reduce load.
**Video:** [How to Box Squat PROPERLY!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nBc_2Jyp3tM)

---

### BB Romanian deadlift (RDL)
*Spreadsheet: "BB RDL"*

**Goal:** Posterior chain strength; hamstring and glute development for overall strength base.
**Cues:** Hinge at hips; bar stays close to legs; neutral spine; feel the hamstring stretch before reversing; soft knee.
**Progress:** Increase weight; single-leg RDL. **Regress:** Reduce load; dumbbell RDL.
**Video:** [RDL Tutorial: 3 Simple Steps](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/5rIqP63yWFg)

---

### Cossack squat
*Spreadsheet: "Cossack squat"*

**Goal:** Lateral hip mobility and adductor flexibility; climbing-specific hip control.
**Cues:** Shift weight fully to one side; working foot flat; extended leg heel on floor; chest upright; don't collapse working knee inward.
**Progress:** Add light weight (goblet hold); pause at bottom; weighted Cossack. **Regress:** Less ROM; hold a post for balance.
**Video:** [Cossack Squat #shorts](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/1EqGnNuUalk)

---

### Elevated pistol squat
*Spreadsheet: "Elevated pistol squat"*

**Goal:** Single-leg strength and balance; climbing-specific unilateral lower-body strength.
**Cues:** Working foot on elevated surface (heel raised); extend free leg forward; controlled descent; push through heel; don't collapse knee.
**Progress:** Remove elevation (full pistol squat); add weight. **Regress:** Box-assisted pistol; band; reduce depth.
**Video:** [Pistol squat (elevated)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-EcoGecB37M)

## 5 · Core

### Toes to bar
*Spreadsheet: "Toes to bar"*

**Goal:** Core compression and hip flexor strength; lat activation at bottom.
**Cues:** Initiate by depressing scapulae; hollow through the movement; touch toes to bar with control; slow lower — don't swing.
**Progress:** Add weight vest; L-sit raises; straight-leg raises. **Regress:** Knee raises; hanging leg raises to parallel.

### Oblique knee raises
*Spreadsheet: "Oblique knee raises"*

**Goal:** Anti-rotation core strength; hip control relevant to climbing body positioning.
**Cues:** From hanging dead hang; rotate hips to bring knees toward one shoulder; control the lowering; hit both sides equally.
**Progress:** Oblique toes-to-bar; add leg extension at top. **Regress:** Reduce ROM; slower tempo.

### Prone Y-T-W raises
*PLAN.md shoulder prehab block*

**Goal:** Lower and mid-trapezius and rotator cuff activation; scapular stabilisation for the planche.
**Cues:** Prone or on incline bench; small ROM; light or no weight; squeeze scapulae together; hold each position 1–2 s.
**Progress:** Add light weight; slow tempo; increase reps. **Regress:** Bodyweight only; reduce range.
**Video:** [Effective 10-Minute Rotator Cuff Training for Climbers](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G-_WiyE9HDg) — Hörst / Training4Climbing

### Face pulls / band pull-aparts
*PLAN.md shoulder prehab block*

**Goal:** Posterior shoulder and rotator cuff balance; counters the forward pulling demand of climbing and OAP work.
**Cues:** Face pulls: band at face height; slight external rotation at end range; no shrug. Pull-aparts: arms straight; pull band to chest height; squeeze scapulae.
**Progress:** Increase band resistance; add W raises. **Regress:** Lighter band; reduce reps.
