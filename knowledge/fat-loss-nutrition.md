# Fat Loss — Strength-First, No Cardio

Shaked dislikes/doesn't have time for cardio. Good news: **fat loss is driven by
nutrition, and muscle is preserved by lifting** — cardio is optional. The plan keeps
all the strength work and changes what happens in the kitchen.

## The 3 levers (in order of impact)
1. **Calorie deficit — small.** ~**300–500 kcal/day** below maintenance. A moderate
   deficit preserves muscle and keeps the OAP/planche progress alive; aggressive cuts
   (800+) burn muscle and stall within weeks.
2. **Protein — high.** **1.8–2.2 g/kg/day → ~150–185 g/day** at 85 kg. Protein preserves
   muscle in a deficit, keeps you full, and has the highest thermic cost. Hit it daily.
3. **NEAT (walking).** Target **~8–10k steps/day**. This is non-exercise movement, not
   "cardio" — it adds meaningful daily burn without eating into recovery.

## Practical guidelines
- Build meals around a **palm-or-two of protein** (meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes,
  whey) + veg + a measured carb/fat portion.
- Keep carbs **around training** to fuel hard sessions and climbing.
- Expect ~**0.3–0.5 kg/week** loss. Faster than that risks strength/skill regression.
- At 85 kg / 175 cm and muscular, body composition will improve faster than the scale —
  track **waist, photos, and lifts**, not just bodyweight.

## Optional "fat-loss finisher" (Saturday Day 5 — strength-style, not running)
10–15 min, high effort, minimal equipment. Pick 2–3 and circuit them:
- Kettlebell swings — 5 × 15
- Loaded carries (farmer/suitcase) — 4 × 30–40 m
- Sled push/drag or weighted step-ups — 5 × 30 s
- Goblet squats — 3 × 15

This raises energy expenditure and conditioning **without** steady-state cardio, and the
loaded carries even reinforce shoulder/scapular stability.

## What NOT to do
- Don't add long cardio — it isn't needed and it competes with strength recovery.
- Don't crash-diet — it will tank the OAP/planche progress, which is the whole point.
- Don't cut protein. It's the one macro to defend.
