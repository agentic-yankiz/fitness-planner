# Goal: Finger Strength — toward a one-hand 20 mm hang (both sides)

Shaked already hangs **two hands on a 14 mm edge with +20 kg** (half-crimp), so finger
strength is *not* the limiter for the one-arm pull-up today. This block exists for a
specific long-term goal: **a one-hand hang on a 20 mm edge, both sides.** That is roughly
a single hand supporting full bodyweight on a comfortable edge — a large jump that builds
the exact grip + connective-tissue tolerance the one-arm pull-up also needs.

> ⚠️ The biggest risk here is the **elbow / finger tendons**, the same tissue the one-arm
> work loads. Ramp slowly, keep it pain-free, and respect the don't-stack rule below.

## The two sessions

### 1. Weighted max hang — Friday (Strength B / push day)
- **What:** 14 mm edge, half-crimp or open-hand, **5 × 7–10 s** at a near-max load,
  **2–3 min** rest between hangs.
- **Where & why:** done **first, on fresh fingers** (a max hang on tired fingers is just
  a sub-max hang). It lives on the **push day** so the heaviest finger work is clear of
  the Tuesday one-arm pulling and is **not** back-to-back with Monday's limit bouldering.
- **Load:** pick a weight where the last 1–2 seconds of a 10 s hang are genuinely hard
  (≈ RPE 8–9). It will start above bodyweight given his +20 kg base.
- **Progress by load**, ~2.5 kg/week across the wave (see `../PLAN.md` progression table).
  Keep the **20 mm edge as the goal**, but train strength on the **14 mm** — strength on
  the smaller edge transfers down to the bigger one.

### 2. Repeaters — Tuesday (Strength A / pull day)
- **What:** 14 mm edge, **bodyweight only** (no added weight at this stage),
  **3 × (7 s on / 3 s off) × 6**, **2–3 min** rest between sets.
- **Where & why:** a **submaximal finisher** *after* the heavy one-arm work, so it never
  steals the priority pulling. Bodyweight on 14 mm is well under his max, so it trains
  **capacity and tissue tolerance**, not peak strength.
- **Leave 2–3 reps in reserve.** If the last set would be a grind, stop — this is volume,
  not a max-out.
- **Progress** by adding a rep per set, then a set, before ever adding weight.

## Path to the one-hand 20 mm hang
A patient ladder — only move up when the current step feels easy and pain-free:

| Step | What | Cue |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Two-hand weighted max hang, 14 mm | Build raw strength (current work) |
| 2 | Two-hand max hang on 20 mm, heavy added weight | Get comfortable owning the goal edge under load |
| 3 | **Assisted** one-hand hang, 20 mm | Pulley counterweight or one or two feet on a chair; remove help over weeks |
| 4 | **One-hand hang, 20 mm** | The goal — train each side, the weaker side sets the dose |

Don't chase the assisted one-hand hang until two-hand max hangs feel comfortable; the
one-arm load on the finger pulleys and medial elbow is the limiter, not motivation.

## Don't-stack rule (read this)
Avoid loading **max finger work + max one-arm pulling on back-to-back days** — it's the
fastest route to a cranky medial elbow or finger-pulley tweak. Concretely:
- Max hang → **Friday only** (away from the Tuesday one-arm day and the Monday limit boulder).
- Repeaters → **Tuesday**, but **submaximal**; if Monday was a hard limit-boulder session
  and fingers/elbows feel cooked, **drop them**.
- This is the same concern flagged in review of the original plan (an optional Monday
  max hang stacked into Tuesday's heavy one-arm pulling) — the dedicated days are placed
  specifically to resolve it. See `training-principles.md` §4.

## Watch-outs
- **Half-crimp, not full-crimp** for loaded hangs — full-crimp adds pulley risk for little
  benefit here.
- Medial-elbow / finger-tendon ache → cut load, keep range of motion, do the prehab; don't
  push through. Re-read `shoulder-physio.md` if the front shoulder also complains.
- Re-test on deload weeks: max-hang load for a clean 10 s, and where you are on the
  one-hand ladder above. Log it in `../tracking/`.

> **Not medical advice.** General strength programming. If a finger or elbow issue lingers,
> see a physiotherapist who works with climbers.
