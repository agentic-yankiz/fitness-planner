# Goal: One-Arm Pull-Up (OAP)

Shaked already pulls **BW +30 kg** (~1.35× BW), so the raw vertical pulling base is
there. The OAP gap is **single-arm recruitment, the lock-off range, and elbow/biceps
tendon tolerance** — that's what this progression targets.

## Master video
- **Hooper's Beta — How to One-Arm Pullup (16 Levels of Difficulty):** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b5gp3QB3rMU
  (climbing physiotherapist; the single best structured progression and the source the
  Tuesday session is modeled on.)

## Progression ladder (low → high)
| Level | Exercise | Cue |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Heavy weighted pull-ups (narrow grip) | Keep building the engine; +30 kg → aim +40 kg over blocks |
| 2 | One-arm dead/active hang | Build grip + shoulder tolerance one-armed (he can already hang +20 kg on 14 mm) |
| 3 | One-arm scapular pulls | Initiate from the shoulder blade, arm straight |
| 4 | Band/towel **uneven** pull-ups | Strong arm on bar, weak hand on towel lower down |
| 5 | **Archer pull-ups** | Working arm bends, other arm straightens along the bar — *the* key driver |
| 6 | Band-**assisted** one-arm pull-ups | Foot/knee in band; remove tension over weeks |
| 7 | **One-arm negatives** | 5–8 s controlled lower; biggest strength builder near the top |
| 8 | **One-arm lock-offs** | Hold at top / 90° / bottom; train each sticking point |
| 9 | **Full one-arm pull-up** | Goal |

## Programming notes
- Train OAP work **fresh** (early in the Tuesday session), submaximal touch on Friday.
- **Elbow armour is non-negotiable:** hammer curls / pronated curls 2–3×/week. The OAP's
  most common injury is medial-elbow / brachialis from the bent-arm load — ramp slowly.
- Progress by **removing assistance** and **adding lock-off time**, not by chasing reps.
- Re-test on deload weeks: max archer reps/side, longest one-arm lock-off, negative tempo.

## Watch-outs
- Front-shoulder pain in the lock-off → reduce range, do prehab, see `shoulder-physio.md`.
- Don't add archer + negatives + lock-offs all at max in the same week; rotate emphasis.
