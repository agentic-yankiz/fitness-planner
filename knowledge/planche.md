# Goal: Planche (working target: straddle planche)

The planche is a **straight-arm, scapular-protraction + anterior-deltoid** strength
feat. Shaked's pulling strength doesn't transfer directly — this is a new, mostly
**pushing/straight-arm** quality plus **wrist** tolerance. Because it loads the
**anterior shoulder and biceps tendon** hard, it must be ramped carefully given the
left-shoulder flag (see `shoulder-physio.md`).

## Progression ladder (low → high)
| Level | Exercise | Cue |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | **Wrist prep** | Mandatory before any planche work — wrists are the early limiter |
| 1 | Scapular protraction push-ups | "Push the floor away," shoulder blades wrap forward |
| 2 | **Planche lean** | Hands by hips, shoulders forward of wrists; lean further = harder |
| 3 | Pseudo planche push-ups | Planche-lean position, add the push |
| 4 | **Tuck planche** | Knees tucked, hips up, arms straight, protracted |
| 5 | Advanced tuck planche | Open the hip angle / flatten the back |
| 6 | One-leg / straddle-in steps | Half-steps between tuck and straddle |
| 7 | **Straddle planche** | Goal |

## Programming notes
- **Straight arms always.** Bent arms turn it into a different (easier, less useful) hold.
- **Protract and depress** the scapula — the serratus work in the prehab block builds this.
- Planche is the **Friday main lift** (trained heavy/fresh); light skill touch isn't needed
  on Tuesday — instead Tuesday's planche lean is kept easy so the front shoulder isn't
  hammered two days running.
- Progress by **increasing lean / opening the tuck / adding hold time**, ~1 step per week.

## Shoulder caution (important)
- Planche leans put large load on the **anterior shoulder + long head of biceps** — exactly
  the area flagged. **Start with a modest lean** and only deepen it when fully pain-free.
- If the front shoulder complains, regress the lean, increase the prehab volume, and
  prioritize the diagnosis from a physio before pushing the lean again.
- Wrist pain → turn hands out slightly, widen base, and keep doing wrist prep.
