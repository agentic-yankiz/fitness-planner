# Training Principles — How this program is built

> For future agents adjusting the plan. The human-facing plan lives in `../PLAN.md`.
> Always reconcile changes here with `../athlete-profile.md`.

## 1. Periodization: 4-week wave + deload
- Block = **4 loading weeks + 1 deload week** (a "5-week mesocycle").
- Loading weeks ramp intensity, not exercise selection: W1 RPE 7 → W4 RPE 9–10.
- **Week 4 is the peak** (highest load/time/least assistance).
- **Week 5 deload** ≈ 50% volume, RPE 5–6 — recover, keep technique grooved.
- Next block **restarts at W1 with new baselines** = the loads/times achieved in W3–W4.
- This is a light **undulating** model: heavy pull (Tue) and heavy push (Fri) hit
  different patterns, and skill work appears submaximally on the opposite day so
  both goals get **2× weekly frequency** (skill grows on frequency, not just intensity).

## 2. Progressive overload levers (in priority order)
1. **Reduce assistance** (band tension, lean angle) on the goal skills.
2. **Add time** to isometric holds (planche, lock-offs).
3. **Add load** to weighted pull-ups (~2.5 kg steps).
4. **Add a set** to main lifts in W3–W4.
Never add more than ~1 lever per week on a given exercise.

## 3. Autoregulation (RPE)
- RPE 7 = 3 reps in reserve, RPE 8 = 2, RPE 9 = 1, RPE 10 = none.
- If sleep/stress is bad, train to the **prescribed RPE**, not the prescribed number —
  the RPE is the real instruction, the kg/seconds are just a guide.

## 4. Why this split (4 days, 2 climbing)
- Goals OAP + planche are **high-skill max-strength** feats → need frequency + freshness.
- Climbing is kept as real training (power on Mon, volume/technique on Thu).
- Heavy pulling (Tue) is placed **after** a climbing day so fingers/elbows are warm but
  the heaviest finger day (Mon limit boulder) has a rest day before it (Sun).
- **Avoid** stacking max finger work + max one-arm pulling on back-to-back days
  (elbow tendons). Hangboarding is now a dedicated block for the **one-hand 20 mm hang**
  goal, but placed to honor this rule: **weighted max hangs on Friday** (push day — away
  from the Tue one-arm day and Mon limit boulder) and **submaximal bodyweight repeaters
  on Tuesday** (a finisher after the one-arm work). A hard limit-boulder Monday means
  lighten or skip Tuesday's repeaters. Details: `finger-strength.md`.

## 5. Recovery & connective tissue
- OAP and planche stress **tendons** (elbow, biceps, anterior shoulder) which adapt
  slower than muscle. The conservative ramp and the prehab block exist for this reason.
- Sleep 7–9 h and the protein target double as recovery tools.
- A tweak in the **front shoulder** or **medial elbow** = back off load, keep ROM, do
  prehab — do not "push through."

## 6. How to evolve the program over cycles
- **Block 2+:** if planche lean is easy at full lean, progress tuck → advanced tuck.
- When **archer pull-ups** are strong with little lean assistance, shift volume toward
  **one-arm negatives → assisted one-arm → lock-off ladders**.
- Re-test benchmarks every deload week (see `../tracking/`).
- Only add the optional **Day 5** when the 4-day week feels fully recovered.

## 7. Fat loss without compromising strength goals
- Keep the deficit **small** (300–500 kcal) so strength/skill keep progressing.
- During an aggressive cut, expect OAP/planche progress to **stall, not regress** —
  that's acceptable. Prioritize protein and sleep. Details: `fat-loss-nutrition.md`.
